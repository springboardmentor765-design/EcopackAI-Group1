Methodology

Ecopack ranks packaging materials by combining machine learning predictions with rule-based product fit, so you see options that are affordable, low‑carbon, and realistic for your product.

1. Data & models

We use a curated dataset of packaging materials (type, strength, recyclability, biodegradability, cost, CO₂) and sample product profiles (size, weight, fragility, category). A Random Forest model predicts cost per kg and an XGBoost model predicts CO₂ per kg for each material–product combination.

2. Product fit scoring

A rule-based score measures how well each material fits your product. It looks at category patterns (electronics vs food vs cosmetics), weight capacity vs product weight, fragility, and whether a material is suitable for liquids, moisture‑sensitive or temperature‑sensitive items. Higher fit means safer and more practical packaging.

3. Cost & CO₂ trade‑off

Model outputs for cost and CO₂ are normalized and combined into an eco‑cost score. Your chosen sustainability level controls the balance: “Standard” weights cost and CO₂ equally, “High” and “Very High” give more weight to CO₂ reduction.

4. Packaging mass estimation

Instead of a fixed ratio, Ecopack estimates packaging mass from your box dimensions, GSM, product category, and fragility. Presets (like kraft or corrugated sizes) or custom dimensions are converted into box surface area and then into mass per unit, with small adjustments for more fragile products.

5. Final ranking & budgets

For each material we compute total cost and total CO₂ for all units, then combine eco‑cost score and product‑fit score into a final score (lower is better). Budget limits per unit add a small penalty if a material is too expensive or unrealistically cheap. The top 5 materials with the best scores are shown as recommendations.

Ecopack is a decision‑support tool: numbers are estimates based on modeled data and should be validated against your real suppliers and packaging tests.
